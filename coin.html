Coin-Toss-Simulation

Description of game constants and values

  • Population:

    The number of participants. Though infact they are all independent players, who don't interact with each other.

  • WinPercent%:

    In each coin toss, if you win, your wealth will grow by this percent.

  • LossPercent%:

    In each coin toss, if you lose, your wealth will shrink by this percent.

  • Rounds:

    How many rounds have been played? A round consists of every player participating in 1 coin toss.

  • PlaySpeed:

    How fast is the simulation running.

  • GiniCoefficient:

    A measure of the inequality of a population. 0 means perfect equality (everyone has the same wealth), 1 means perfect oligarchy (everyone has no money, except one person who has all the wealth)

  • TotalWealth: Sum of each player's money. Initially the same as populationSize * moneyPerPlayer. If the "expected value" of each coin toss is > 0, then the total wealth will keep growing.
