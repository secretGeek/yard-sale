Yard-Sale-Model with Income Tax and Universal Income

Description of game constants and values

  • Population:

    The number of participants

  • MaxBet%:

    In each trade (or gamble) the amount being bet is equal to the MaxBet% of the poorer player. For example, if we choose "20%" as the max betting percentage, and the two players betting have wealth of $100 and $1 million dollars, respectively, then the "stakes" they are gambling for would be $20 as this is 20% of the poorer player's wealth.

  • TaxationRate%:

    If the winner of a trade has more wealth than the median user, then this amount of tax is collected from the winnings. At the end of each round, the collected winnings are distributed equally to all players

  • Rounds:

    How many rounds have been played? A round consists of every player participating in 1 bet.

  • PlaySpeed:

    How fast is the simulation running.

  • GiniCoefficient:

    A measure of the inequality of a population. 0 means perfect equality (everyone has the same wealth), 1 means perfect oligarchy (everyone has no money, except one person who has all the wealth)

  • TotalWealth: Sum of each player's money. Initially the same as populationSize * moneyPerPlayer. If trades are zero sum, then this stays constant.
